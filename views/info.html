This website is an interactive learning platform for the transport layer security (TLS) protocol's handhake, version 1.3. It is intended for educational purposes.

Two tools are available:

  • TLS Handshake: Discover how the TLS protocol works by visualising and interactively playing with the fields and steps of the protocol
  • Attacks: Discover possible attacks (downgrade and replay) in TLS by visualising them with animation.

1. TLS Handshake

This tool helps the user to play around the protocol and discover each field and step of the protocol.

The TLS Handshake sub-protocol can be divided into three phases (key exchange, server parameters, and authentication), where each phase has many steps and each step has many fields. The three phases (key exchange, server parameters, and authentication) are represented by the three colors: Blue, Orange , and Green respectively. Blocked Step:

When the step has a grey background, this means that the step is not yet done. The blocking of a step can be due to:
  • Normal reasons: the user did not yet arrive to the step
  • Alert: blocking of the step because of an alert that was triggered from a step preceding it.

Alert:

When an alert is triggered, the handshake is aborted. Therefore the tool will go back to the step where the alert was triggered and block all the steps following it.

To unblock the steps that were blocked due to an alert, the alert must be resolved.
